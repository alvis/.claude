      How should a completed merge be stored?

      The redirect, in one query

      # FeedbackThread#canonical — follow the tombstone to the live thread
def canonical
  merged_into_id ? FeedbackThread.find(merged_into_id).canonical : self
end

      Trade-offs, honestly

      Wins

      • Redirect tombstone keeps every read one hop away — old permalinks to B resolve through a single indexed column.
      • The additive migration is safe to deploy ahead of the feature and reversible on its own.

      Costs

      • A tombstone stores only the last merge target; a chain of merges is walkable but the intermediate history is thin.
      • Reversing a tombstone means clearing two columns and re-splitting votes — recoverable, but not audited by default.

      Fails when

      • You need a queryable audit of who merged what and when — that is exactly what the ledger alternative buys, at the cost of a join on every canonical lookup.

      Step 2 · Product behaviour Most likely to change

      Merging two threads has to combine their votes and followers.

      When B folds into A, their signal has to add up — but "add up" hides a real product call. If the same customer voted on both threads, is that one voice or two? And should everyone following B start getting A's updates without asking? There is no neutral default here, so this is yours to set. app/models/feedback_thread.rb:44‑71

      Worked example illustrative

      Thread A has 38 votes, thread B has 21, and 9 customers voted on both. Dedupe → the survivor shows 50. Raw sum → it shows 59, with those 9 counted twice. sample numbers

      How should votes and followers combine on a merge?

      Trade-offs, honestly

      Wins

      • Deduping keeps the merged vote count defensible when you quote it back to the customer or to the roadmap.
      • Auto-following means the people who cared about B hear when A ships — the whole point of merging.

      Costs

      • Dedupe needs a distinct-by-customer query at merge time; on a giant thread that is a few hundred milliseconds, not free.
      • Auto-following generates a burst of notifications the follower never explicitly opted into.

      Fails when

      • A customer following B considers the auto-subscribe spam — the third option exists precisely for teams who expect that complaint.

      Step 3 · Permissions Likely to change

      Merging is destructive-ish, so who gets to do it matters.

      A merge quietly rewrites which thread customers see and where their votes land. Today anyone with triager access can edit a thread. I proposed keeping merge at that same bar because merges are reversible — but if you consider a merge weightier than an edit, raise it. app/policies/feedback_thread_policy.rb:18‑40

      Who should be allowed to merge threads?

      Reversibility, either way

      Every option above keeps merges reversible for 30 days: a reversal restores B, re-splits the votes recorded at merge time, and re-subscribes B's original followers. The permission choice only changes who holds that undo, not whether it exists.

      Author's own cuts

      What this plan deliberately leaves out.

      These are my non-goals and soft spots for this pass — not the cost of any one option, which lives in each step's trade-offs. Read them before you treat the plan as complete.

      Left out on purpose

      • No automatic duplicate detection. A human still decides two threads are the same; suggesting merges from text similarity is a separate, bigger project.
      • No bulk merge. This ships one-at-a-time merges only; clearing a backlog of duplicates in one action is noted as a follow-up, not built.
      • No cross-product merges. Threads can only merge within the same product — merging across products raises permission questions I did not want to answer implicitly.

      Weakest part of this plan

      • Merge chains. If A is merged into B and later B into C, the redirect-tombstone option walks the chain correctly but stores no summary of it — deep chains are rare but the plan does not prove they stay cheap to resolve.
      • The "9 customers voted on both" figure in step 2 is an illustrative shape for showing the dedupe math, not a measured overlap rate — it must not be used to size anything. overlap rate
