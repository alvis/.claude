Five material findings

Each risk carries evidence, consequence, and a probe.

Observed · authorizationAUTH-07

Revocation and derived-cache invalidation are separate paths.

Evidence. The permission consumer clears retrieval keys; the summary worker keeps its evidence bundle until TTL expiry.

Why it bites. A user can lose access and still receive a generated claim derived from the revoked record for up to fifteen minutes.

Cheapest probe. Revoke access between retrieval and render, then inspect cache keys and the returned citation set.

Identity platform
Observed · lineageTRACE-03

The audit event stores the summary, not the evidence snapshot.

Evidence. summary_generated records case ID, model, and output hash but omits evidence IDs and authorization version.

Why it bites. During an incident, operators cannot distinguish a model error from an authorization race or reproduce what the model saw.

Cheapest probe. Reconstruct ten sampled summaries using audit data alone and record which inputs are missing.

Trust engineering
Inference · trustEVAL-12

Citations can be present while the claim overstates the source.

Evidence. The current evaluator checks citation existence and lexical overlap, not whether uncertainty or chronology was preserved.

Why it bites. A qualified agent note can become a definitive customer-facing diagnosis while still pointing to the correct record.

Cheapest probe. Grade fifty summaries for entailment, uncertainty preservation, and temporal ordering with disagreement review.

Applied quality
Observed · resilienceOPS-04

The safe fallback has never been exercised under partial failure.

Evidence. Staging tests disable generation before requests begin; none interrupt generation after evidence retrieval.

Why it bites. The containment path may fail precisely when timeouts and stale evidence pressure are highest.

Cheapest probe. Inject timeouts at retrieval, generation, citation assembly, and portal render; verify source-only output at every cut.

Support operations
Hypothesis · responseHYP-02

Human review may become the hidden throughput limit.

Basis. The proposed first cohort produces about 1,800 summaries per day; the current escalation rotation reviews fewer than 120.

Why it bites. A control described as “human in the loop” can silently degrade into sampling with no stated coverage or priority rule.

Cheapest probe. Run a one-day shadow queue and measure review demand by severity, latency, and duplicate rate.
