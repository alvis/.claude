Five gates

A score is useful only when every gate is inspectable.

PassingG1 · behavior

Caller contract

Criterion. Inputs, externally visible outcomes, idempotency, and failure propagation are known for every caller.

Evidence. Five call paths traced; contract table includes synchronous and queued callers.

Gap. Delivery-latency acceptance remains implicit but does not change architecture.

Exit: latency budget added to acceptance
PassingG2 · data

Persistence boundary

Criterion. Template, attempt, idempotency, and audit ownership are traceable across migration stages.

Evidence. Tables, writers, readers, retention, and foreign-key dependencies are mapped.

Gap. Analytics replica is deferred with data-platform ownership.

Exit: deferred owner recorded
Passing with follow-upG3 · operability

Failure and recovery

Criterion. Retry, deduplication, replay, alerting, and rollback behavior are explicit.

Evidence. Worker claims, provider errors, and operator replay were walked against the runbook.

Gap. No automated replay UX; manual replay is accepted for the first extraction stage.

Exit: rollback drill before cutover
BlockingG4 · ownership

Preference enforcement

Criterion. One owner controls unsubscribe, legal suppression, channel preference, and audit semantics.

Evidence. Callers resolve product preferences; the facade applies global suppression.

Gap. No decision says whether the extracted service owns product-specific policy.

Exit: owner and API consequence decided
Needs acceptanceG5 · rollout

Migration and rollback

Criterion. Dual-run comparison, cohort order, mismatch threshold, and rollback owner are agreed.

Evidence. Shadow-delivery design is feasible without changing callers.

Gap. Product has not accepted the mismatch threshold or ramp duration.

Exit: acceptance owner signs thresholds
